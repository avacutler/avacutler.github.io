About

Ava Cutler is a mathematician and writer, born and raised in New York City. She moved to Vienna, Austria in June 2023 where she works at the at the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA). At the agency she assists with yearly reports and programming. Some programming projects include a ChatBot used to identify complex and unsearchable information specific to IAEA teams, improvements to data manipulation software used for report production, and an online calculator to simplify access to International Target Values (ITVs) based on complex variables.

Before the IAEA, Ava was a Software Developer and Consultant at FAST Enterprises, LLC. She developed software for two state government agencies, projects that modernized the Tennessee Unemployment Insurance and New Hampshire Tax systems.

In 2021, Ava graduated from Macalester College with a degree in Mathematics and a minor in English. While there, she found a passion for problem solving in many forms. In her coursework, she enjoyed writing proofs and manipulating equations to produce a desired solution. After class, she tutored peers in math and physics at the Macalester Academic Excellence (MAX) Center, often helping students with problems she had never seen before. In her three years as a tutor, she rose to the highest student position. In the second year she became a Supervisor to other tutors, and then STEM Tutor Representative, overseeing other tutoring sessions and working closely with MAX Center leadership to improve the tutoring Center.

She studied abroad in Rabat, Morocco in 2020, a time that was abruptly cut short due to the Covid-19 pandemic. Still, she lived in the city for 2 months, taking courses in French, and writing articles for Morocco World News. She was able to continue her writing even after returning to the United States, and continued a relationship with the team throughout university.

Ava has lived in 5 cities since 2020, and in each she found joy in new hobbies that connect her to the place she resides. In Minnesota, she took up cross country skiing. In Rabat, she explored the local cuisine. In New Hampshire, she hiked and horseback rode. In Tennessee, she explored the live music scene, all while testing her tolerance to hot chicken.
