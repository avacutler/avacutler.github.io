About

I am an Implementation Consultant at Fast Enterprises, currently living and working in Concord, New Hampshire. Here, I develop and configure software for the New Hampshire Department of Revenue Administration (DRA). The product is used daily by DRA staff, taxpayers and various third parties.

I recently graduated from Macalester College with a Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics and a Minor in English. At Mac, I spent two summers working closely with Professor William Mitchell to develop research in Fluid Mechanics. For the first project, we studied polynomial interpolation as a subset of a larger project developing an integral equation solver based on overlapping patches. The second project was funded by the National Science Foundation, and investigated the fluid motion of a helical ring. This ring is part of how the microorganism, Dinoflagellate, moves through highly viscous fluid. We modeled the ring in Python and Mathematica and used Stokes' equations to investigate how such a strange shape could help an organism navigate.

During the school year, I worked at the Macalester Academic Excellence (MAX) Center as a math and physics tutor. I worked closely with students helping them with problem sets, study for exams, and simply better understand concepts they were learning in their classes. I was also a Supervisor and STEM representative at the MAX Center, overseeing other tutoring sessions, and working closely with MAX Center leadership to improve the Center and discuss how to help tutors and students get the most out of our work.
