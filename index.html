At Mac, I spent two summers working closely with professor William Mitchell to develop research in Fluid Mechanics. For the first project, we studied polynomial interpolation as a subset of a larger project developing an integral equation solver based on overlapping patches. The second project was funded by the National Science Foundation, and investigated the fluid motion of a helical ring. This ring is part of how the microorganism, Dinoflagellate, moves through highly viscous fluid. We modeled the ring in Python and Mathmatica and used Stokes' equations to investigate how such a strange shape could help an organism navigate.
