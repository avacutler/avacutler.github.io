About

I am a young professional passionate about problem solving, literature, and social justice. Presently, I am a software Implementation Consultant at Fast Enterprises, LLC, where I develop software and consult with the client on-site at the New Hampshire Department of Revenue Administration (DRA). The product is used daily by DRA staff, taxpayers, and various third parties. In New Hampshire, I have found joy going on hikes, taking horseback riding lessons, and re-reading the Harry Potter series on my porch. Originally from New York City, I have never been able to enjoy the fruits of nature as regularly as I can now.

I graduated from Macalester College in 2021 with a degree in Mathematics and a minor in English. While there, I found a passion for problem solving in many forms. In my classes, I enjoyed writing proofs and manipulating equations to get to a desired solution. After class, I tutored my peers in math and physics, often helping students with problems I had never seen before. I learned to simultaneously guide them through a problem and explain concepts while attempting to solve it on my own. I was also a Supervisor and Representative at the MAX Center, overseeing other tutoring sessions and working closely with MAX Center leadership to improve the Center and discuss how to help tutors and students can get the most out of our work.

Over the summer I worked closely with Professor William Mitchell to participate in two Fluid Mechanics research projects. In the first summer, we optimized a polynomial interpolation solution for specific functions, which would later work into a larger project, developing an integral equation solver based on overlapping patches. In a second summer, in an NSF-funded project, we researched the fluid motion of an immersed helical ring, focusing on the microorganism dinoflagellate which uses this shape to move. For both projects, I used Python, MATLAB and Mathematica extensively to both solve equations and plot functions.
