About

Ava Cutler is a Mathematician, Software Developer, Writer and Artist. Born and raised in New York City, she moved to Vienna, Austria in June 2023 where she works at the at the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA). At the agency, she specializes in mathematics, programming and data analysis.

Before the IAEA, Ava was a Software Developer and Consultant at FAST Enterprises, LLC. She developed software for two state government agencies, projects that modernized the Tennessee unemployment insurance and New Hampshire tax systems.

In 2021, Ava graduated from Macalester College with a degree in Mathematics and a minor in English. While there, she found a passion for problem solving in many forms. In her coursework, she enjoyed writing proofs and manipulating equations to produce a desired mathematical solution. After class, she tutored peers in math and physics at the Macalester Academic Excellence (MAX) Center, often helping students with problems she had never seen before. In her three years as a tutor, she rose to the highest student position.

Ava studied abroad in Rabat, Morocco, in 2020, an excursion that was abruptly ceased due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Still, she lived in the city for two months, taking courses in French and writing articles for Morocco World News. She continued to contribute to the paper even after returning to the United States and maintained a relationship with the team throughout university.

In each of the five cities Ava has lived in since 2020, she found joy in new hobbies that connected her to the place she inhabited. In Minnesota, she took up cross-country skiing. In Rabat, she explored the local cuisine. In New Hampshire, she hiked and horseback rode. In Tennessee, she grew to love live music while testing her tolerance to hot chicken.
